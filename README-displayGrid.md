# displayGrid

Turn your latest social media posts into a clean, embeddable grid for your own website.

**displayGrid** is a lightweight app that connects to a user's social media content, pulls their latest approved posts, and displays them on their own webpage — perfect for About pages, creator pages, brand sites, portfolios, and landing pages.

## What It Does

displayGrid helps users showcase their newest social content on their own website without manually updating image galleries every time they post.

The app is built for a simple use case:

- connect a supported social account
- fetch the user's latest posts
- display them in a clean grid on the user's webpage
- keep the website feeling current with minimal manual effort

## Why displayGrid

Most users already post regularly on social media, but their personal site or business site often looks outdated. displayGrid solves that by making it easy to reuse recent content on their own webpage.

This is useful for:

- creators
- photographers
- personal brands
- small businesses
- agencies
- real estate professionals
- restaurants and local businesses
- anyone who wants fresh content on their website without constant manual edits

## Core Features

- Pulls a user's latest social media post data
- Displays posts in a website-friendly grid layout
- Useful for About Us sections, portfolio areas, and homepage content blocks
- Simple integration for personal or business websites
- Designed to reduce manual content updates
- Built to be lightweight and easy to use

## Typical Use Case

A user connects their social account and allows displayGrid to access the latest approved post data. The app then retrieves that content and places it into a visual grid that can be shown on the user's own webpage.

Example use cases include:

- showing latest posts on an About page
- adding a live-looking social strip to a homepage
- building a portfolio grid from recent content
- keeping a brand website fresh without manual uploads

## Privacy and Data Handling

displayGrid only uses the information needed to retrieve and display a user's authorized social media content.

Depending on the integration method, this may include:

- basic account information
- public media/post data
- authentication tokens or connection credentials needed to maintain the authorized connection

displayGrid should use secure authentication methods wherever possible and should avoid storing raw passwords directly.

For more information, see the project privacy policy and delete-data instructions.
